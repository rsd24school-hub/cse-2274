Types of pillars and columns

Doric

Origin: Ancient Greece.

Characteristics: The most simple and oldest Greek order. It features a stout, unadorned, and simple circular capital and rests directly on the floor without a base in Greek design.

Ionic

Origin: Ancient Greece.

Characteristics: Recognizable by the volutes (scrolls) on its capital. Ionic columns are generally thinner and fluted compared to the Doric.

Corinthian

Origin: Ancient Greece.

Characteristics: The most ornate of the three Greek orders. Its capital is elaborately decorated with acanthus leaves, flowers, and other scrolls.

Tuscan

Origin: Ancient Rome.

Characteristics: A simpler, Roman version of the Doric column. It typically lacks ornamentation and has a simple base and unadorned capital.

Composite

Origin: Ancient Rome.

Characteristics: A Roman order that combines elements from other styles. It blends the volutes of the Ionic capital with the elaborate acanthus leaves of the Corinthian capital.
