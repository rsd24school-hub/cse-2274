Gallery of Pillars and Columns (This page is a work in progess)

This gallery showcases various types of architectural pillars and columns, highlighting their unique designs and historical significance.

Doric Column

The Doric order is the simplest and oldest of the classical orders, characterized by its sturdy proportions and plain capital.

Ionic Column

The Ionic order is known for its elegance and scroll-like volutes on the capital, often used in more decorative architecture.

Corinthian Column

The Corinthian order is the most ornate, featuring elaborate capitals decorated with acanthus leaves and scrolls.

Tuscan Column

The Tuscan order is a simplified version of the Doric, with unadorned capitals and bases, often used in Roman architecture.

Composite Column

The Composite order combines elements of the Ionic and Corinthian orders, featuring volutes and acanthus leaves in its design.
