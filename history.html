History of Pillars and Columns

This page explores the history of architectural pillars and columns, from ancient Greek Doric, Ionic, and Corinthian styles to Roman Tuscan and Composite orders.

Doric

The Doric column is the most simple and oldest Greek order, featuring a stout, unadorned circular capital and resting directly on the floor without a base.

Ionic

The Ionic column is recognizable by the volutes (scrolls) on its capital. Ionic columns are generally thinner and fluted compared to the Doric.

Corinthian

The Corinthian column is the most ornate of the Greek orders. Its capital is elaborately decorated with acanthus leaves and scrolls.

Tuscan

The Tuscan column is a simpler Roman version of the Doric column, typically with a simple base and unadorned capital.

Composite

The Composite column is a Roman order that combines elements from other styles, blending Ionic volutes with Corinthian acanthus leaves.
